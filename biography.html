Biography

Native of Russian Federation, Vasily Yurin has established himself as a virtuoso classical guitarist who plays a very high level repertoire. In 2013 he was awarded first prize in the Performance Excellence Competition at the University of Central Florida competing against performers in all instrumental categories. In January 2010 he won the Artist Competition at the Winter Music Symposium at the University of North Florida, Jacksonville.

Vasily began playing classical guitar at the age of nine years under the guidance of Maestro Tatiana Sabangieva. After successfully graduating from music school, he decided to devote himself to music and continued education studying guitar performance, music theory, history, conducting, instruments of orchestra and orchestration, as well as pedagogy disciplines, in the class of Maestro Yuri Lihachev at the Rostov State Music Pedagogic College, Russia. After graduating with the honors diploma, Vasily was offered admission to the prestigious Rachmaninoff Conservatory in Southern Russia. At the age of eighteen he was hired by the Rostov Regional Philharmonic Society as an artist of the ensemble of Russian folk instruments “Dontcy”. The ensemble would perform over 80 concerts annually across the nation, toured Germany and Italy.

Vasily is working on his second music degree in the United States. In 2012 he graduated from Valencia Community College where he studied with Dr. Troy Gifford in Music Performance program. Vasily was offered numerous scholarships and two out-of-state tuition waivers, from Eastern Europe Linkage Institute and College of Arts and Humanities, which allowed him to study Guitar Performance at University of Central Florida with Dr. Eladio Scharrón. In the Fall of 2014 he completed Bachelor’s studies at UCF and in the Spring 2018 received Master of Arts degree.
